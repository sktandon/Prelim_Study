Key Information:

You are invited to participate in this study that will contribute to PhD thesis research taking place at King's College London. Before you decide whether you want to take part, it is important for you to understand why the research is being done and what your participation will involve.
Please take the time to read the following information carefully and discuss it with others if you wish.

What is the purpose of the study? The goal of the study is to understand how data visualization is percieved by adult individuals (18 years and older) and how information visualization can be improved depending on individual differences.

What will happen if I take part? After filling in a demographic questionnaire, you will begin par one, where you will play a game while observing visualizations. You will then be asked to interact with the visual to recall what you saw. In part two, you will be asked to play a paper folding game, and finally asked to rate the difficulty of each part.

Duration and Privacy: The survey will take you approximately 15 minutes to complete. Your responses will be kept completely anonymous and we will note store any information that could be used to establish your identity. Any data we collect will be stored securely on our servers. Demographic information gathering is for research analysis and will not be identifiable. We take your security very seriously.

Do I have to take part? Participation is completely voluntary. You should only take part if you want to, and choosing not to take part will not disadvantage you in any way. You are free to withdraw at any point during the completion of the survey, without having to give a reason by simply not submitting the survey. We believe this study presents no risks to you. Once you submit the survey, it will no longer be possible to withdraw from the study because the data will be anonymous.

Contact Information: If you have any questions, require more information about this study, or think you have been harmed by participating please contact the primary researcher using the following contact details:
